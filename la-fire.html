Latest Updates

January 12, 2025: Emergency response teams have contained the fire, but over 20 families are still displaced. Temporary shelters are operating at capacity.

January 13, 2025: Firefighters in Los Angeles County are bracing for another round of powerful winds as they continue to battle the wildfires.

The Palisades blaze remains the largest, burning for over a week. A new fire in neighboring Ventura County has led to more evacuation orders.

The total area burned by the Palisades, Eaton, and Hurst fires is approximately 16,425 hectares (40,588 acres), an area larger than Paris.

Death tolls from the wildfires are devastating, with at least 25 lives lost. The Eaton fire is now the deadliest wildfire in Southern California's history, claiming 17 lives.
